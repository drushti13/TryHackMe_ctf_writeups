# Searchlight - IMINT

This CTF focused on image-based OSINT (IMINT), where the objective was to identify locations and answer a series of questions using only the information available in the provided images. It required paying attention to small details and combining multiple OSINT techniques to narrow down the correct answers.

For each challenge, I started by carefully examining the image for visible clues such as landmarks, road signs, building names, text, vehicle registration plates, vegetation, and other unique features. Even small details often helped narrow down the search.

I primarily used **Yandex Search** for reverse image searching, as I found it returned better matches than other search engines for location-based images. I also used **Google Lens** to identify landmarks, extract text from images, and gather additional context when needed.

When reverse image search wasn't enough, I used **Google Dorking** and normal Google searches with different keywords to find websites, articles, or images related to the location.

## Tools Used

* Yandex Search
* Google Lens
* Google Search
* Google Dorking


This challenge showed me how multiple OSINT techniques can be combined to solve image-based investigations. Rather than relying on a single tool, I learned to verify information from different sources and use small visual clues to identify locations accurately.
